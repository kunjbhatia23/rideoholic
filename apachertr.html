Description

The TVS Apache RTR 160 is a renowned and agile sport-oriented motorcycle that delivers an exciting riding experience. Powered by a 159.7cc single-cylinder engine, it combines power and efficiency, making it ideal for both city commuting and spirited rides. The "RTR" in its name stands for "Racing Throttle Response," emphasizing its sporty nature. With its race-inspired styling, sharp design lines, and LED headlamp, the Apache RTR 160 boasts an aggressive and attractive look. Its chassis and suspension are tuned for superior handling and precise control, ensuring an enjoyable ride on twisty roads. The digital instrument cluster provides essential information at a glance. Whether you're a performance enthusiast or a daily commuter, the TVS Apache RTR 160 offers a perfect blend of style, power, and handling, making it a popular choice in the 160cc motorcycle category.

TVS Apache RTR 160



TVS Apache RTR 160 is a street bike available in 3 variants and 5 colours. Apache RTR 160 price starts at Rs. 1,44,218 in India with top variant price starting from Rs. 1,51,568. The TVS Apache RTR 160 is powered by 159.7cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 15.82 bhp and a torque of 13.85 Nm. With both front and rear disc brakes, TVS Apache RTR 160 comes up with anti-locking braking system. This Apache RTR 160 bike weighs 138 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 12 liters.

TVS Motor Company has launched the 2022 Apache RTR 160 in the Indian market. The latest iteration of the motorcycle is available in three variants – Drum, Disc, and Disc with Bluetooth – and five colours Gloss Black, Pearl White, Racing Red, Matte Blue, and T-Grey.

The base model uses a drum brake setup at the rear wheel and a single-channel ABS. The mid and the top versions get disc brakes on both ends along with dual-channel ABS. The top-of-the-line version further benefits from SmartXonnect technology with the voice assist function. The prices vary depending on the variant selected.

Apart from new features, the 2022 model also gets a few styling tweaks. The styling revisions include redesigned and sharper-looking headlight and taillight. This model also gets an X-ring chain and a wider 120mm rear tyre, and is 2kg lighter than its predecessor. Meanwhile, the 2022 version retains the fuel tank, engine cowl, single-piece seat, split-style grab rail, and side-slung exhaust from the previous model. The hardware comprises telescopic front forks and twin springs to handle suspension tasks.

The feature list now comprises an LED headlight with LED DRL at the front instead of a halogen headlight on the old model. At the back, the 2022 model gets a redesigned LED taillight. Other features include a Bluetooth-enabled fully-digital instrument cluster with revised user interface, slipper clutch, dual-channel ABS, and three ride modes – Rain, Urban, and Sport.

The mechanical specifications retain the 159.7cc, air-cooled, single-cylinder engine. Linked to a five-speed gearbox, the motor now makes 15.8bhp at 8,750rpm and 13.85Nm of peak torque at 7,000rpm as against 15.3bhp and 13.9Nm previously.

The TVS Apache RTR 160 rivals the Bajaj Pulsar NS160, Pulsar N160, Hero Xtreme 160R, and the likes in the Indian market.
