Description

The Yamaha FZS series represents a line of stylish and versatile motorcycles designed for urban riders. These bikes, such as the Yamaha FZS V3 (as of my last knowledge update in January 2022), are known for their 149cc air-cooled, single-cylinder engines that offer a perfect balance of power and efficiency. The FZS series features modern and muscular designs with LED lighting, sculpted fuel tanks, and sharp lines that create a bold and attractive presence on the road. With its upright and comfortable riding position, the FZS is well-suited for daily commuting and short trips. Yamaha often updates its models, so there may be newer versions available beyond my last update. Nevertheless, the FZS series is recognized for its practicality, style, and reliability, making it a popular choice for riders looking for a versatile and good-looking motorcycle for urban use.

Yamaha FZS V4 Summary



Yamaha FZS Fi V4 is a street bike available in only 1 variant and 5 colours. FZS Fi V4 price starts at Rs. 1,47,302 in India. The Yamaha FZS Fi V4 is powered by 149cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 12.2 bhp and a torque of 13.3 Nm. With both front and rear disc brakes, Yamaha FZS Fi V4 comes up with anti-locking braking system. This FZS Fi V4 bike weighs 136 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 13 liters.

The Yamaha FZ S V4 is available in a single variant- Deluxe. It features a fascia that is different from the V3 and gets LED turn indicators as well. Apart from that, the styling remains largely unchanged and the V4 has carried over the large fuel tank, side panels, and overall profile. The FZ S V4 also comes with an LCD console that houses a speedometer, odometer, trip meter, fuel level readout, and smartphone connectivity.

Other standard features on both variants include an LED taillight, Bluetooth connectivity, a multi-function LCD instrument cluster, an LED headlight, a tyre hugging rear mudguard, and a lower engine guard. The FZS-Fi Dlx further benefits from LED flashers. The Dlx variant also gets different graphics, coloured alloy wheels, and a dual-tone seat. That said, Yamaha has also equipped the motorcycle with a traction control system as standard.

It continues to draw power from the same 149cc single-cylinder motor with the output rated at 12.2 bhp and 13.3 Nm. Itâ€™s mated to a five-speed gearbox. The conventional front forks and monoshock at the rear on the FZ S Fi V4 are the same as the previous model. Even the front and rear disc brake setup remain unchanged. Moreover, Yamaha claims to make all its bikes ethanol compliant by the end of this year.

The Yamaha FZ S Fi V4 is offered in three paint schemes: Metallic Grey, Majesty Red and Metallic Black.
